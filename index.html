I'm Carlos.

a former clinical psychologist delving into neuroscience and computational psychiatry.

Hi.

I'm a PhD student working with Prof Oliver Robinson at the Institute of Cognitive Neuroscience at UCL in London, UK. I am studying the neural and computational mechanisms underlying pathological anxiety and its treatments.

My Interests.

I'm interested in emotional disorders and how we can use computational modelling - particularly reinforcement learning (RL) - to understand the mechanims that explain the diversity of patient trajectories as well as their treatment. At a more personal level, I like reading, photography, history and I am a bit of technology enthusiast. Arch Linux user.

My Skills.

Data analysis

Univariate and multivariate statistical analysis using GLM. Machine learning methods canonical correlation analysis (CCA).

Neuroimaging

Preprocessing and analysis of task-based fMRI and dMRI with FSL. Some experience with NiPreps such as fMRIPrep.

Coding

R, Python, Stan, Matlab, Unix Shell, Markdown, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. You can see some of my code on my GitHub. Also, I built this very website.

Computational modelling

Computational modelling for behaviour and cognition. Particularly, I have used reinforcement models (RL) to model decision-making data.

Languages

I speak English, Spanish and French. I can speak a bit of Portuguese as well.

IT and others

I know enough to know how to exit vim and not to try running "rm -rf /*"   with superuser privileges. Also, I have years of experience as a CBT therapist.
